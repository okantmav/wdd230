Aguascalientes is a state in central Mexico. It is bordered by Zacatecas to the north and west, Jalisco to the south, and San Luis Potosi to the east. The capital of Aguascalientes is also named Aguascalientes.

The state has a population of 1,425,607 as of 2020. The majority of the population is of mestizo (mixed European and indigenous) descent. The official language is Spanish, but there are also a number of indigenous languages spoken in the state, including Nahuatl, Tepehuan, and Pame.

The economy of Aguascalientes is based on manufacturing, agriculture, and tourism. The state is home to a number of automotive, aerospace, and electronics manufacturing plants. Agriculture is also an important part of the economy, with the state producing corn, beans, wheat, and fruits. Tourism is another important industry, with the state attracting visitors to its colonial-era architecture, thermal springs, and racetrack.

Here are some statistical information about Aguascalientes:

  • Population: 1,425,607 (2020)
  • Area: 5,618 square kilometers
  • Capital: Aguascalientes
  • Official language: Spanish
  • GDP per capita: US$16,000 (2020)
  • Unemployment rate: 3.5% (2020)
  • Life expectancy: 77 years (2020)
  • Literacy rate: 98% (2020)

Aguascalientes is a prosperous state with a strong economy and a high quality of life. It is a popular destination for business and tourism, and its residents enjoy a safe and comfortable environment.
